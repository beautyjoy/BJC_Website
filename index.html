The Mission: ESL for Silicon Valley's immigrant population

Silicon Valley (Santa Clara and San Mateo counties) is currently home to over 175 thousand Spanish-speaking adults who identify their English language skills as insufficient for full participation in their greater community. However, the current supply of high-quality educational programs for adult English language learners (ELLs) in Silicon Valley is limited to serving only five to seven thousand learners, irrespective of their first language. Our mission is to close this drastic gap between the huge demand and limited supply of quality adult ESL instruction in Silicon Valley.

Our Approach: Borderless classrooms for adult ELLs

Through a careful blend of cutting edge language pedagogy and cutting edge learning technologies, we are developing a scalable model that will meet the needs of the full population of Spanish speaking adult ELLs throughout Silicon Valley. Our blended curriculum will leverage current resources in the community in order to provide high quality and flexible English language instruction both when and where our target learners need it the most. We do not intend to replace existing location-based efforts, but rather to provide them with the means to drastically increase their current offerings.

The platform: ESLX

Rather than building a platform from scratch, we are making use of edX, an online learning platform developed and supported by a coalition of major research universities headed by Harvard and MIT, but also including our local institutions, UC Berkeley and Stanford. edX has already proven itself effective in supporting university level education, and our meticulous exploration of the platform has confirmed that it can be customized to also meet the needs of adult, Spanish speaking ELLs. We refer to our customized version of the platform as ESLX: edX modified specifically for ESL instruction.
